Letter: Inflation debate — what goes around comes around
From William McClain, Rockville, MD, US

Loretta Mester, president and chief executive officer of Federal Reserve Bank of Cleveland © Melissa Lyttle/Bloomberg

Published SEP 30 2022

Saturday’s Big Read (“The escalating fight against inflation”, FT Weekend, September 24) quotes
Christian Keller of Barclays Investment Bank as saying the Federal Reserve has become “afraid of
losing control of the [inflation] process”.
I recall attending a symposium with Janet Yellen, Ben Bernanke, Olivier Blanchard, Loretta Mester
and others at a Washington think-tank in 2019 where the topic was “missing” inflation after the
Great Recession.
Then the issue was a decade of below-target inflation despite highly accommodative policy. Now
we may face stubbornly high inflation despite aggressive rate raising. Perhaps then as now, our
central banks don’t have as much control over the “inflation process” as we’d like to imagine?

William McClain
Rockville, MD, US
