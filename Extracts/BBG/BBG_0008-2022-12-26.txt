Gold rose — trading above $1,800 an ounce — as China swept away more of
its Covid-19 controls, piling pressure on the dollar as risk assets gained.
Read more: Hedge fund managers boost net bullish gold bets to six-month
high
China will no longer subject inbound travelers to quarantine from early
January, adding to the rapid rollback of strict pandemic rules and boosting
sentiment toward Asia’s biggest economy.
Markets are also digesting US data on Friday that pointed to softer
inflation and slower consumer spending, which could ease pressure on
the Federal Reserve for more rate hikes
Gold has gained more than 9% this quarter, helped by the greenback’s
retreat and hopes that the Fed will slow aggressive monetary tightening
Spot gold rose 1.7% to $1,828.86 an ounce as of 10:39 a.m. in New York on
Tuesday; the Bloomberg Dollar Index dropped 0.3% and is close to its
lowest since June. Silver and palladium were higher while platinum
slipped
Separately, copper in New York gained 2.4%
