Copper Climbs as Traders Weigh Fed Outlook Before
Inflation Data

By Jessica Zhou
December 16, 2025 at 10:29 PM EST
Updated on December 17, 2025 at 1:06 PM EST
Save

Translate

1:11

Copper gained as investors await US inflation data that could impact the
Federal Reserve’s stance on further interest-rate cuts.
Prices rose as much as 2% on the London Metal Exchange on Wednesday,
ahead of the key economic report due Thursday. Several Fed officials are
due to speak publicly, with Chicago Fed President Austan Goolsbee saying he
expects

strong growth and further rate cuts next year.

The industrial metal has rallied more than 30% this year after a series of
mine disruptions cut supply. The market continues to draw support from
resilient demand tied to electrification and grid investment, which has kept
underlying sentiment constructive.
Still, copper has fluctuated since hitting a record of almost $12,000 a ton on
Friday. This week’s uneven performance underscores the market’s
vulnerability to shifts in sentiment, including any cooling of optimism
around artificial intelligence or economic data that could dampen broader
risk appetite.
The metal traded up 1.2% to settle at $11,737 a ton on the LME. All other
metals gained, with aluminum rising 1% and tin up 3%.
